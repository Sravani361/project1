Watchmen

Alan Moore and Dave Gibbons

Watchmen by Alan Moore and Dave Gibbons is a groundbreaking graphic novel set in an alternate 1985 where superheroes exist but are largely marginalized. The story begins with the murder of Edward Morgan Blake, also known as the Comedian, prompting a group of retired heroes to investigate the circumstances. As they delve deeper, they uncover a conspiracy that threatens global destruction. The narrative intertwines complex characters, each grappling with their own moral dilemmas and the implications of power.

Fun Home

Alison Bechdel

Fun Home: A Family Tragicomic by Alison Bechdel is a poignant graphic memoir that explores the author's complex relationship with her father, Bruce Bechdel, a closeted gay man who ran a funeral home. Through a blend of personal narrative and literary analysis, Bechdel recounts her childhood experiences, her coming out as a lesbian, and the impact of her father's secretive life and tragic death. The graphic novel is notable for its intricate artwork and its exploration of themes such as identity, sexuality, and family dynamics.
