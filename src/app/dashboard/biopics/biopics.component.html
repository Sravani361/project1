M.K. Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi, born on October 2,1869,in Porbandar,India,was a key leader in the Indian independence against British rule. Known for his philosophy "satyagraha," he advocated for civil rights, social reforms,and peaceful protests. Gandhi's commitment to truth and justice inspired movements worldwide, making him a symbol of peace and resilience. His efforts led to India gaining independence in 1947. He was assassinated on January 30, 1948.

The Diary of a Young Girl

Anne Frank

"The Diary of a Young Girl" is the powerful account of Anne Frank,a Jewish teenager who went into hiding during World War II to escape the Nazis. Written between 1942 and 1944, the diary captures her thoughts, dreams, and struggles while living in a concealed annex in Amsterdam. Through her introspective writing, Anne reflects on the challenges of adolescence, her hopes for the future, and the harsh realities of war. Her diary ultimately serves as a profound testament to the resilience of the human spirit and the enduring impact of her story continues to resonate globally.

Long Walk to Freedom

Nelson Mandela

"Long Walk to Freedom" is the autobiography of Nelson Mandela, chronicling his journey from a rural village in South Africa to becoming a pivotal leader in the fight against apartheid. Published in 1994, the book details his early life, education, and the development of his political consciousness. Mandela shares his experiences as a prisoner for 27 years, his commitment to nonviolent resistance, and the eventual negotiations that led to the end of apartheid. This powerful narrative not only highlights his personal struggles but also reflects the broader struggle for justice and equality in South Africa. It remains a symbol of hope and resilience.
