The Haunting of Hill House

Shirley Jackson

The Haunting of Hill House by Shirley Jackson is a psychological horror novel centered on a mysterious mansion known for its dark history and unsettling atmosphere. The story follows four characters—Dr. John Montague, an investigator of paranormal phenomena; Eleanor Vance, a shy woman seeking a sense of belonging; Theodora, a bohemian artist; and Luke Sanderson, the heir to Hill House—who gather at the estate to explore its supernatural occurrences. As the haunting intensifies, the characters confront their own fears and vulnerabilities, leading to chilling revelations.

House of Leaves

Henry James

House of Leaves by Mark Z. Danielewski is a complex and experimental horror novel that intertwines multiple narratives. The main story follows Johnny Truant, who discovers an unfinished manuscript about a documentary film called The Navidson Record, which chronicles a family living in a house that is larger on the inside than it is on the outside. As the family explores the strange, shifting spaces within the house, they encounter increasingly unsettling phenomena. The narrative is presented in a unique format, with unconventional typography and footnotes that create a layered reading experience.

Bird Box

Josh Malerman

Bird Box by Josh Malerman is a post-apocalyptic thriller that follows Malorie, a mother trying to protect her two children from a mysterious entity that drives people to madness and suicide upon sight. Set in a world where the threat is unseen, the survivors must navigate their environment blindfolded to avoid looking at the creatures. The story alternates between Malorie’s desperate journey downriver to safety and flashbacks to the events leading up to the catastrophe.
